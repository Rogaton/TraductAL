## Usage Tips

### Personalization Checklist

Before sending any template:
- [ ] Replace [Name]/[Organization] with actual recipient
- [ ] Customize [their research topic] / [their domain]
- [ ] Add 2-3 sentences showing you know their work
- [ ] Update [Your Name], [Your Email], [Repository URL]
- [ ] Adjust tone for audience (formal for government, casual for startups)

### Timing

**Best times to send**:
- Universities: Beginning of semester (August/September, January/February)
- Government: After budget cycles
- NGOs: Anytime, but avoid December holidays
- Conferences: 2-3 months before submission deadlines

### Follow-Up

If no response in:
- 7-10 days: Polite follow-up
- 2 weeks: Consider them not interested
- Exception: Very senior academics (may take longer)

### Tracking

Keep a spreadsheet:
- Date sent
- Recipient
- Response (yes/no/pending)
- Follow-up actions
- Outcome

---

## Batch Sending Strategy

### Phase 1 (Week 1): Test Recipients
- 5-10 people you know personally
- Get feedback on email content
- Refine based on responses

### Phase 2 (Week 2-3): Targeted Outreach
- 20-30 top-priority contacts (universities, key researchers)
- Personalize each email
- Monitor response rates

### Phase 3 (Week 4+): Broader Announcement
- Mailing lists (ACL, LREC, etc.)
- Social media
- Academic forums
- Less personalized, wider reach

---

**Remember**: Quality over quantity. 10 personalized emails to the right people beat 100 generic blasts.
